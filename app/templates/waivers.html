Waiver Wire Recommendations {{ page_info('waivers') }}

These recommendations are tailored to your league's H2H Points scoring. The waiver wire is where you win your league, and in a points format the value landscape looks different than in roto. Three types of players are especially valuable here that standard rankings often miss:

Closers & Setup Men

A save is worth 7 points and a hold is worth 4. A closer who gets 3 save chances in a week and converts cleanly can generate 37+ points from a single roster spot. Even a setup man with 2 holds per week quietly produces 20+ points. These players show a high Fit score.

Contact Hitters (Low K%)

Every strikeout costs -0.5 points. Over a season, the difference between a 15% K hitter and a 28% K hitter is roughly 35 points — equivalent to 9 extra home runs. Players with high plate discipline and low K rates are systematically undervalued on most waiver wires.

Innings Eaters

Each inning pitched generates 4.5 base points from outs before anything else. A starter averaging 6.5 IP/start collects 29 points from outs alone per start. Even a boring 5.50-ERA guy who eats innings can be worth a streaming spot if the matchup is right and the alternative is an empty slot.
