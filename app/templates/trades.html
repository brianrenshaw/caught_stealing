Trade Analyzer {{ page_info('trade-analyzer') }}

All trade values are calculated using your league's H2H Points scoring. Unlike generic fantasy rankings, this analysis accounts for the specific point values that make your league unique. Closers are worth far more here than in most formats (SV = 7 pts), and high-strikeout hitters carry a hidden penalty (K = -0.5 pts). A player's Projected Points is their estimated rest-of-season fantasy point total, while Surplus Value shows how many points they're worth above a freely available replacement at the same position — the number that actually matters for trades.

Trade Value Rankings {{ page_info('trade-value-rankings') }}

Players ranked by Surplus Value — the projected rest-of-season fantasy points above what a replacement-level player at the same position would produce. This is the single most important number for evaluating trades: a positive surplus means the player is worth more than what's freely available on waivers, and the higher the number, the more you should demand in return.

Note that relievers often rank surprisingly high because saves are worth 7 points each in this format. An elite closer producing 35 saves generates 245 points from saves alone — that's hard to replace. Similarly, innings-eating starters rank well because every IP generates 4.5 base points from outs. Don't trade your closer for a middling bat just because traditional rankings say the hitter is "better."
